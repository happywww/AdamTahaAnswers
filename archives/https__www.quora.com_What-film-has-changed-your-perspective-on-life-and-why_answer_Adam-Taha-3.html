I love French movies. I watch them a lot. I can’t understand the language as much but I can feel the story. I was fortunate someone subtitled the movie so it was easy enough to follow. Nearly missed this gem…

Les Choristes

This movie is close to my heart because a musician, arrives at a French boarding school for "troubled" boys, to work as a supervisor. I did the same for a boarding school.

I came back to the city after touring as a musician and singer, and wanted to do something I always dreamed of doing. Inspire the young students with words and music. I found myself in same situation as the character in movie. I didn’t realise the forces I was going against in the system.

The boarding school was ruled by iron first where young students didn’t get much empathy, if at all.

Mathieu (the new supervisor, who wants to help the young boys) discovers the boys being ruthlessly punished by the headmaster Rachin.

So he attempts to use humour and kindness to win them over, help them learn music, sing, to grow in respect and hope in humanity.

The lessons in the movie reminded me of what I learnt from the students and the environment…

  1. One person can make a difference in the lives of many.
  2. A word of encouragement may not seem like it can change a life but if you continue to say it, they grow to believe in themselves.
  3. To gain respect you got to give respect to the young and understand much of what’s in their mind is a product of their environment.
  4. To not take much of what they say personally but to be patient, kind, hopeful and to never give up on them as others have.
  5. Maturity isn’t about age. Some were very mature for their age. Avoid talking down at them. Talk with them as human being and they will grow to do the same in time to come.
  6. They can learn to grow in confidence, in respecting themselves and others. It takes work, consistency and belief that it is all worth it
  7. Sometimes no matter what you do, it seems you don’t make any difference but keep going in the hope it does.
  8. You got to believe in them more than they believe in themselves.
  9. Music can be used as a meduim but the objective results is humanity.
  10. You got to do it more for a wage because it will challenge you mentally, emotionally and spiritually. Sometimes you will meet parents and it be heartbreaking to see the other side of the young’s life and parents too.
  11. You may never achieve fame in this area. Your work maybe unsung. Others will take the credit but no matter. You’ll touch and change young lives in the most fundamental way.
  12. You may not be looked up to by the world but these students will look up to you through the ages of time. Maybe not now, but in time, they may remember your words and find courage in themselves.
  13. It is sad, perhaps even tragic, that the teacher in this movie considers himself a failure, and indeed this may call in to question the standards by which society judges its heroes. I saw many who felt this way but I saw their parents and many teachers as heroes.
  14. The day I left after some years, as the character in the movie left, I came to the same realisation. You can’t change the system. Some people in there are too old to change and some too late to change.
  15. You don’t change the world. You inspire, impact by example one person at a time. Only by the willing cooperation did the students want to learn, believe and grow.

Quote from the movie..

Clément Mathieu: Never say never. Something's always worth trying.
