Originally Answered: Should I be an artist ?

First I would say, you need to decide what you want to be and when you know, when it burns you inside, it doesn't matter what challenges you got or what experience or skill you haven't got.

It will propel you forward.

However, I need to say something which most artist leave far too late and simply don't do enough of.

You need to be an artist who has the marketing and sales mind.

Today as an artist you need to be the:

  1. Marketer
  2. Sales rep
  3. PR
  4. The blogger
  5. The social connector
  6. The Podcastor
  7. etc etc.

You see, to be an artist and even a great one isn't going to really help you make a living as an artist. If that's what you want to do. Actually, strike that. To be whatever you want to be and be good at it is...not enough.

Don't let anyone scare you that making money from your talent isn't do-able. That's a myth. Many artists are personal bs themselves when they say this. I'll say why below...

It takes marketing and sales.

I have a friend who is brillaint painter but he didn't sell much paintings. He's more of an introvert guy, isolating himself, draeing and painting...painting.

He has no clue whatsoever about marketing and generating sales from his work.

I'm an artist and I am not even close to this guy. I swear, this guy if we put like a percentrage is like 80% better than me at what he does. The man is just so damn good at what he does.

But he has a hard time selling his work.

Then there's me. This is one of my works....


It's not that great but it does the job but it is not what makes the money. What sells it is the marketing, the concept behind it and the years of work it took to build reach and reputation with many other artists and their customers too. Plus my own customers.

I had to learn these skills

  • Strategy
  • Marketing
  • Selling and consumer psychology
  • PR

I am now so busy mate, I have to build a website so I had to also add more skills like...

  • Building partnerships with web developers.
  • Advertising > PPC > Facebook Advertisng > Solo Ads
  • Copywriting
  • Vide production
  • Audio podcast
  • etc etc..

There are many, many artists that can do better than me. Juan Pablo Foerero showed you some and they would kick my ass when it comes to drawing and painting.

So if you're worried about being the best. If you're worried about competition. If you're worried about how on earth can you win in such a crowded marketing - don't be.

You first figure out what it is you want to become.

Make a definite decision.

Then align yourself with someone out there who likes your work and has marketing and sales skills. Someone who had done it before and can help you with these things.

I had to learn myself and it tooks years mate but I wouldn't want it any other way because from it, it has helped me to see what can be possible. I had to see it for my own eyes and now, all that talk about..

"Oooh, to be an artist, and make money, is sooo hard, it just doesn't happen because..." is now nonsense.

I know the truth now from seeing what can be done.

The problem wasn't competition or how someone was better.

It was because years ago I had no idea how to market and sell what I do in an attractive, influencing, motivating and persuading way - and in a scalable way that frees me instead of enslaves me.

Keep practising your art, your skill but also learn how to be a marketer, think about a concept or a way that is unique to position yourself within a target market.

Hope this inspires you and gives you some food for thought.
